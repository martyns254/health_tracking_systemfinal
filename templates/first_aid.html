Bleeding and Wounds

+
1 Clean your hands and wear gloves if available
2 Apply direct pressure using clean cloth or gauze
3 Keep pressure until bleeding stops (usually 10-15 minutes)
Seek immediate medical attention if:
  • Bleeding doesn't stop after 15 minutes of pressure
  • The wound is deep or gaping
  • There's an embedded object

Burns

+
1 Cool the burn under cold running water for at least 10 minutes
2 Remove any jewelry or tight items near the burned area
3 Cover with clean, non-stick bandage
Seek immediate medical attention if:
  • The burn is larger than your palm
  • It's a chemical or electrical burn
  • The burn affects face, hands, feet, or genitals

Choking

+
1 Encourage the person to cough
2 Give up to 5 back blows between shoulder blades
3 If unsuccessful, perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
Call emergency services immediately if:
  • Person cannot speak, cough, or breathe
  • Person becomes unconscious
